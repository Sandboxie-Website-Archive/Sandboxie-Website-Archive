Post by Craig@Invincea » Sun Apr 10, 2016 8:16 pm

On a ancient core i7, 16GB ram, Win 10x64 FR14316 build, Comcast Xfinity Norton Security Suite (and Norton Outlook addon.)

*from Clicking on Outlook 2016, until seeing the "outlook loading" box, almost 4 seconds. (Outlook preconfigured w/ @Live email account)
*When opened in SBIE (Dragging Outlook 2016) into SBIE Control, that box took almost 5 seconds. (and that was with an Office Update dl in the background for both tests..)
Outlook Forced 5-6 seconds.

Word in SBIE (Run from task notification menu) =8 seconds.
Word (w/o SBIE) 2-3 seconds
Word Forced=7.5 seconds.(there was a noticeable delay w/ Word.)

I watched everything load in SBIE, Start, RCPSS, etc..etc. then Word.exe

You also have to remember that SBIE isn't taking advantage of faster startups that running Office in Windows would have. SBIE has to run through it's startup processes while loading Word/Outlook inside a Sandboxed environment.

And you may see BETTER performance from single (if they're still around) and dual core systems than you would multiple cores. A few years back, a typical quad-core would always lose to a dual-core in single-threaded performance scenarios, but that has changed somewhat in brand new Intel chips.


This was non VM environment.

I spoke with one of the Invincea Dev's and the Invincea Support folks, there is known "lag" in Office when it comes to the container. They've customized some .XML files, reviewed client requirements and thus geared any performance bumps tweaked per the client (mainly Enterprise clients).

Some things they noted:
Lync running in the background w/ Office Docs, and causing delays....o they excluded Lync in various ways.
Another issue was McAfee for one client that had to be tweaked to to play nice, etc.
Blocking OneDrive (as it didn't meet many clients need for data security in the Cloud.)

The Invincea product doesn't support Win 10, so they have that going for them
