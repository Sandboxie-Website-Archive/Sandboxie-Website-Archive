Hello bassmastergg,

We do not recommend using Sandboxes to store files (however, many users do).
You have to make sure that Auto-Delete is not selected, since as soon as you delete the contents of the Sandbox, the folder will be gone as well.
Also, make sure other programs aren't deleting the Sandbox contents, for example, CCleaner tends to do that.

If you can provide more information regarding what exactly are you trying to do, and what are the errors you encounter, we might be able to best assist you.

Hello bassmastergg,

Bo's suggestion is the best way to go. The folder has already been recovered, so it is on your host. Moving it to another location won't change much, except that you can easily empty it by deleting the contents of your Sandbox.

You can move it inside a Sandbox folder if you really want to, just cut it and paste it inside your desired Sandbox (default Sandbox location is C:\Sandbox\Username\SandboxName\)

If you are encountering a specific issue when trying to move the folder, please provide the full error message and the steps to reproduce it.
