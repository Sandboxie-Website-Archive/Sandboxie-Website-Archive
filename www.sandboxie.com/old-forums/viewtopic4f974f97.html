Dear Forum Members

I believe that this has drifted off the main reason for it coming into existence, i.e. the problem of sandboxing Firefox caused by a NAV/NIS update on 19th October. And at the moment, there is no known solution other than removing NAV/NIS and it's associated files from your PC (or Tablet).

However, my main bone of contention is how destructive some Symantec updates can be, it seems like these updates deliberately target other non-Symantec protective applications, let me relay the problems:-

An update, released in July, which upgraded NIS from 21.x to 22.5x did the following

a) Deleted four non-Norton utilities, two of which were protective apps.
b) Tried to remove the connective software that allows my PC to communicate with my NAS.
c) Damaged the "Temp File Deletion" facility in Norton Utility 16, this facility can no longer delete temp files, stop counting the non-deletion of temp files or complete it's now non-existent function (i.e. stop and pass on to the next function/facility in the NU list).

And what makes this worse is that while Symantec/Norton have gone on to repair NAV/NIS after this debacle but they have not fixed or updated Norton Utility 16 yet.

My next problem, as known to all thread readers, is the nonsense with another Norton update crippling the ability of Sandboxie to open Firefox, another couple of non-Norton protective applications.

For me, there is no such thing has having too much protection on your PC and so I am so pi??ed off with Norton mainly attacking non-Norton protective applications, it is quite obvious that for whatever reason Symantec/Norton is now releasing under-tested and potentially destructive updates and this for me is totally unacceptable.

Admittedly, NAV/NIS seem to offer the best all round protection, much better than other AVs out there, but this attack on other products have me wondering whether I should move onto another Internet Security product.


UK Bob
