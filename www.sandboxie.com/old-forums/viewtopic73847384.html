I have reccomended Sandboxie to several of my friends. I think it is a phenomenal tool. My only worry is that if it becomes "mainstream", then the hackers are going to start targeting it.

So in addition to being a great tool (the best in my security suite), part of the appeal of the program to me is that it is not mainstream yet and probably not being targeted by hackers.

I am not a security expert, but I would think that Hackers and malware writers would try to get the most bang for their buck by targeting the most popular programs from the most popular vendors (Microsoft, McAffee, Norton, Webroot,etc.).

So as much as I feel that this program should be on everyone's PC, part of me doesn't want to see that.
