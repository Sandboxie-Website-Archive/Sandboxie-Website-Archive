Post by RizingPower » Thu Jan 15, 2015 3:52 pm

I have been using 3.76 for well over 2 years with no problems so far. Why? The last time I tried upgrading post 3.76, it's as though Kaspersky suddenly stopped working. I upgraded to 4.14 a few days ago and today, I must have mistyped a URL by accident since I was redirected to a fake AV website that claimed I was infected. I deleted the contents of the sandboxie right away, but felt truly dissapointed that Kaspersky anti-banner did NOT block the site or ad, like 3.76 does. In fact, upon initially upgrading to 4.14, I found out that sites that normally did not show ads suddenly showed ads.

After downgrading to 3.76 and revisiting the same sites do the ads dissapear again. Initially I thought this was a Kaspersky problem, but it is apparently a sandboxie problem. Downgrading sandboxie, not kaspersky, caused my normal kaspersky internet protection shields to work again.

I rely on sandboxie to prevent permanent damage from happening to my computer, but rely on Kaspersky to prevent sandboxie from resorting to such drastic measures to begin with, with sandboxie acting as a fail safe. The ability of sandboxie to complement KIS really shakes my trust in post 3.76 releases, no matter how many nice features they add to it.

So now I'm wondering, what the hell did they change? 3.76 is currently the only safe release for me, kaspersky URL and Anti-banner protection wise.
