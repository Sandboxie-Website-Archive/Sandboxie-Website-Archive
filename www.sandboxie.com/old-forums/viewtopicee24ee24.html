Interesting. I think I've been using AMM/AWM longer than I have sandboxie. Unlike technoid however, for me it's usually just to control separate backgrounds on the monitors and an extended taskbar these days. I've never had issues with AWM before (with other software) aside from the standard bugs and occasional broken features. For the sake of testing however I uninstalled AWM from my system and replace it with a trial of DisplayFusion and updated SBIE to 5.12 again. So far the issues I noticed in my short stint with 5.12 before haven't popped up again.

Some down sides of switching to DisplayFusion:
It's a .NET program, I'm not fond of these and avoid them when possible.
As a .NET program it is much larger in memory than all the processes of AWM combined.
I've noticed the DisplayFusion.exe tends to use more cpu in the background than all the processes of AWM combined. Every couple seconds it's using 1-6% of the cpu and only drops to <0.01 for short periods.
I don't have a license for it.

Some positive aspects of DisplayFusion:
Has an option to disable application hooks! [love it]
It's working well with Sandboxie 5.12
It lets me do everything I still used AWM for.
It has ASLR!

Normally I'd be a tad more irate about the suggestion of dropping AWM or sticking to an older version of SBIE but seeing how I am hoping to move to Win10 around the redstone release [they finally fixed most of the issues I had] anyhow and Win10 lets me handle the things I need natively - it will be a moot point for me soon. So grr anyway but whatever, this setup will be an acceptable workaround till then. =(

Thanks for the testing though, I wonder if it could be something that just needs changed/fixed inside AWM that wasn't exposed before now?
