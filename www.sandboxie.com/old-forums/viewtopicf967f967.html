Hi. Might I suggest that for the poor newbies who are either screen-reader users like myself, or get distracted and miss it, you somehow postpone the redirect a bit longer after someone submits a post? The first time I posted my screen reader was plunked at the bottom of the page. By the time I got to the top and jumped down to look for a message saying my topic had been posted, there was no such thing. I couldn't find my post either. I went to "view your posts", and no results were found. So I hit the back button and resubmitted the post. This time I was a bit faster and read the message saying my post was submitted, but awaiting moderation. As soon as I read that, it was gone, replaced by the main page of the subforum I was on. If you must redirect the page automatically instead of the usual click OK to continue, a good 40 second delay would be good. A screen reader only reads a page one line at a time, though you can skip a lot of junk by jumping by headings or lists or other markers. But you have to learn the layout of the page first before you can find things quickly on a new site.
